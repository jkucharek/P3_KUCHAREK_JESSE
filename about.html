Welcome to ACME

Though never clearly define in Road Runner cartoons, ACME conglomerate produces every product type imaginable, no matter how elaborate or extravagant. In the Road Runner cartoon "Beep, Beep," we revealed our full company name to be "Acme Rocket-Powered Products, Inc." based in Fairfield, New Jersey. Many of our products were produced specifically for Wile E. Coyote. For example, the ACME Giant Rubber Band, subtitled "For Tripping Road Runners."

Sometimes, ACME can also send living creatures through the mail, though that isn't done very often. Two examples of this are the ACME Wild-Cat, which was once used on Elmer Fudd and Sam Sheepdog; and ACME Bumblebees in one-fifth bottles.

In addition to our products being of the utmost quality, ACME delivery service is second to none; one can drop an order into a mailbox (or enter an order on our website), and have the product in his or her hands within seconds. As part of our commitment to excellence, we are constantly expanding our selection, reviewing customer feedback, and never ceasing to deliver the quality products synonymous with the ACME name.

About our Owner

In his past, our owner, Wile E. Coyote (Canis Iatrans), would often obtain complex, some would say ludicrous, gizmos from our mail-order division in hopes of helping him to catch The Road Runner (Geococcyx californianus). Though never able to successfully catch The Road Runner, Wile E. knew this not to be the fault of any ACME products, as he recognized the superior quality which ACME represents. It is for this reason that Wile E. took a job working for ACME, though he did not come forward to reveal this publicly until 2003. At first utilizing his position to facilitate the purchase of ACME products with use of his employee discount, it was not long before Wile E. realized that he could satiate his hunger with the salary he earned as an employee of the ACME Corporation.

Not long after this realization, and getting on in years, Wile E. determined it best to focus on his career with ACME, and, possessing unparalleled knowledge of ACME’s products, steadily rose through the ranks until eventually acquiring enough capital to purchase the company outright.

Not one to bow out, Wile E. has seen to it that his protégé Calamity Coyote has access to an unlimited ACME credit card account in hopes that he may one day see his former efforts come to fruition vicariously. It is unknown whether he is aware that the company he now owns is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Roadrunner Corporation.
